sidewalks around the world are filled with smartphone zombies, and i'm here to clean our streets. that was the message painted on the side of the apple store in chicago's hip bronzeville neighborhood. the message was signed the brick-phone bandit. reports have been flooding in that people who walk down the street while texting have had their smartphones snatched by a middle-aged white male dressed in 1990s cross-color outfits.  he then hands them a working old-school brick cell phone from the '90s, with a charger, no less. according to chicago detective amy shanker , the stolen phones are returned a week or so later with a note - keep your eyes on real life. and they're always signed, the brick-phone bandit. so far, 14 people in chicago have been victims of the bandit. his latest victim had their apple watch stolen and replaced with a sundial.
